1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing that I paid attention to was the fact that the page was initially split by the four neon colours. It was a coincidence that my mouse had also happened to be at the intersection of the four versions of text, which immediately prompted me to move the mouse around in attempt to read the various versions of the text where the keywords hard interchanged. This initial interaction had also encouraged me to hover over all the different elements on screen moving forward, setting the fun tone in the interactions for the rest of the page.

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time on the landing page in attempt to read what the texts say under the influence of the different colours. This was primarily due to finding the words in the pink and pruple difficult to read, so I had to spend more time in order to decipher the words. Otherwise, I spent the longest actually interacting with the webpage with the pixelised image. I just found it mesmerising and addictive to play with. After the initial addictive play, I was then interacting out of curoisity to come up with ideas as to how it might have been coded to create that effect.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action was definitely was moving the mouse around the screen, sometimes randomly, but othertimes over elements on the screen ge to see if and how they react after the initial realisation on the landing page. It became some what obvious what I should do as I realised a pattern to how the interactions were displayed throughout the portfolio website; interchanging between mouse tracking and hover based interaction. Though a pattern can be identified, each section showed the viewer a different possibility of interaction design even though the core action was the same/similar.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The impression gathered from the website was to showcase the artist's interactive design / capabilities as a portfolio piece to share with people in cases of job searching and networking.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The artist communicates the primary goal by turning the portfolio itself into a piece of work, rather than adding work to showcase. This gives the viewer a more intimate experience to their abilities rather than having seperate things to interact with. It makes it effective and concise.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The impression is that it is designed to be a quick and easy interaction experience; to convey the full potential artist's skills set in the shortest amount of time. This takes into account the fact that when applying for jobs, the hiring party would most likely be looking through many portfolios. Condensing the time required to interact with the portfolio would allow for the hiring team to to pay more attention to more than just a mere glance. It may invite them to interact with the webpage for longer than required.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

It prompts the user's curiosity. Whether that is in the form of playing around with the webpage more or to look further into the artist's other socials to see what else they have done apart from the webpage. As a hirer it may prompt the webpage to be shared with others given its simple and concise nature. In a networking context, it may be a conversational prompt, prompting more interest in the artist's other works.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The work takes root from Graphic Design concepts. Particularly with the dramatic landing page utilising tetradic colours, this makes it particularly attention grabing, pulling the user in at a glance. Making it hard to dismiss. Alongside colour theory, Tuntaopas uses a combination of interaction and shapes to create optical illusions which make up the imagery on their portfolio. This is evident in the 'perfectly center[ed]' triangle and 'pixel displacement effect' landscape image.

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

This provides an example to how skills and techniques from different practices may cross over, and emphasises the importance of having an open mind to broadening your scope in terms of learning, and it's not a negative thing to learn things outside of design. Those skills, techniques and ideas may one day cross over to your work and create something unique.

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

This promotes the importance of curiosity as a designer as much as open mindedness. Feeling curious as a designer to reach out to other venues and practices will only provoke further intellectual thinking, improving one's own practices when it comes to creating work. Combining keeping an open mind and curiosity will only push ones' work to improve and create opportunities for them to reach their creative potentials.

12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The primary frustration of the interaction would be the colour choices for the pink and purple squares on the landing page. The chosen contrasting colour for the highlighted keyword in those squares doesn't provide a great contrast level and made readability low. It had taken a moment longer to read what those squares had highlighted, unlike the green and blue squares which were readable at a glance. Otherwise, the other interactions were really fun, and readability was high.

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying moment was realising everything interacted upon mouse location/mouse tracking. It made the website very enjoyable to explore despite being quite short in comparison to the other portfolio websites available. I found the text button interaction at the end of the page particularly enjoyable to interact with. The way the text would spin and move around, and exchange and transform letters made it fun to hover over, whilst efficiently showcasing skill capability. I found that particularly smart on top of the experience as a front end web developer's portfolio.
